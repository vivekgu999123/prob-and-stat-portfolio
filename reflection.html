Reflection

The course Fundamentals of Probability and Statistics helped me understand how data can be analyzed and interpreted systematically. I learned to summarize datasets using measures of central tendency and dispersion, and to visualize data using graphs such as histograms and box plots.

The study of probability concepts and distributions improved my ability to reason under uncertainty and model real-world problems. Topics like hypothesis testing and confidence intervals gave me a clear understanding of how conclusions about populations can be drawn from sample data.

Overall, this subject strengthened my analytical thinking and provided a strong foundation for data-driven applications in computer science.
