Units and Chapters

Chapter 1: Description of Data

Introduces statistical data, types of variables, and numerical measures such as mean, median, mode, variance, and standard deviation.

Graphical tools including histograms and box plots are used to visualize data distribution, variability, and skewness.

What I Learned

From this chapter, I learned how to classify data and summarize it using appropriate statistical measures such as mean, median, mode, variance, and standard deviation. I also learned how graphical tools like histograms and box plots help in understanding data distribution, variability, and skewness.

Chapter 2: Probability

Covers fundamental probability concepts, addition and multiplication rules, conditional probability, and Bayes’ theorem.

Emphasis is placed on modeling uncertainty and decision-making using probabilistic reasoning.

What I Learned

This chapter helped me understand probability as a mathematical way of handling uncertainty. I learned to apply addition and multiplication rules, conditional probability, and Bayes’ theorem to solve real-world and engineering-related problems.

Chapter 3: Random Variables and Probability Distributions

Introduces discrete and continuous random variables and probability distributions such as Binomial, Poisson, and Normal.

Mean, variance, PDF, CDF, and graphical interpretation of distributions are studied.

What I Learned

From this chapter, I learned how random variables are used to represent outcomes of random experiments. I gained understanding of discrete and continuous distributions and learned to apply Binomial, Poisson, and Normal distributions to model practical situations.

Chapter 4: Statistical Inference

Focuses on sampling methods, confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing for means and proportions.

Z-tests and t-tests are applied to draw conclusions about populations from sample data.

What I Learned

This chapter taught me how conclusions about a population can be drawn using sample data. I learned the concepts of sampling, confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing, and how Z-tests and t-tests are used to make data-driven decisions.
